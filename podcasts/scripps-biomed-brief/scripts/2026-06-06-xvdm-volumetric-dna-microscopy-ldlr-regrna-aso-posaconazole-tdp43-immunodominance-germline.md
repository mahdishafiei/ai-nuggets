# Scripps Biomedical Brief — 2026-06-06

## Script

Good morning. Today's brief: cross-linked volumetric D-N-A microscopy for dense molecular-network phenotyping in intact three-dimensional tissue from Joshua Weinstein's group at the University of Chicago, antisense oligonucleotides targeting a regulatory R-N-A at the L-D-L receptor locus to increase L-D-L-receptor expression and lower L-D-L cholesterol in vivo from a CAMP4 Therapeutics team led by Daniel Tardiff, posaconazole as a C-N-S-penetrant CYP fifty-one inhibitor that clears cellular T-D-P forty-three pathology from Jonathan Sachs's group at the University of Minnesota, and a systems-scale structural model showing that germline-encoded antibody features rather than somatic mutations drive immunodominance from Ellen Shrock, Stephen Elledge and David Baker's groups at Harvard and the Institute for Protein Design.

First, from Nianchao Qian, Rabia Yasser and colleagues with Joshua Weinstein at the University of Chicago, cross-linked volumetric D-N-A microscopy — xVDM — a sequencing-only method that builds dense, three-dimensional molecular networks in intact tissue without a microscope. Resolving cell phenotypes in full tissue context has required imaging methods that retain spatial neighborhoods, but they trade off depth, throughput, and the number of biomolecules they can capture. xVDM seeds unique molecular identifiers directly into the tissue's protein matrix and links them with uniquely labeled D-N-A bridges, generating a D-N-A-encoded proximity network from which cell-scale molecular communities are reconstructed computationally. Nucleating networks from the protein matrix produces denser graphs and broader transcriptome recovery than methods that nucleate from transcripts alone. The team mapped genetically annotated three-dimensional networks in intact zebrafish embryos at twelve, eighteen, and twenty-four hours post-fertilization, and extended the same framework to human tonsil with antibody-oligonucleotide conjugates. The whole pipeline runs on standard bench reagents and a D-N-A sequencer — a low-equipment-barrier substrate for three-dimensional molecular phenotyping.

Second, from Keerthana Gnanapradeepan, Sylvia Manska and colleagues with Daniel Tardiff at CAMP4 Therapeutics, a working example of a novel antisense oligonucleotide modality that increases endogenous gene expression by targeting non-coding regulatory R-N-As at promoters and enhancers. The team mapped regulatory R-N-As at the L-D-L receptor locus in HepG2 cells and human liver using epigenomic profiling and a bespoke regulatory-R-N-A capture sequencing method, designed antisense oligonucleotides against the actionable elements, and showed that several increased L-D-L-receptor messenger R-N-A and protein in cells. A lead oligonucleotide administered to a humanized-liver mouse model raised human L-D-L-receptor messenger R-N-A and protein and lowered plasma L-D-L cholesterol — the in vivo translation step that has been the rate-limiter for this whole class of expression-increasing oligonucleotides. The framework — regulatory-element mapping, oligonucleotide screening against actionable sequences, humanized in-vivo validation — generalizes directly to haploinsufficient disease loci where restoring half-dose expression is the therapeutic goal.

Third, from Nathan Kochen, Sahar Zafari and colleagues with Jonathan Sachs at the University of Minnesota, posaconazole — an F-D-A-approved, C-N-S-penetrant, orally delivered antifungal — emerges as a leading candidate to clear cellular T-D-P forty-three pathology relevant to A-L-S and frontotemporal dementia. The group had previously shown that ketoconazole, an antifungal CYP fifty-one inhibitor, stabilized T-D-P forty-three native self-interactions and reduced pathology, but ketoconazole's liver toxicity blocks A-L-S repurposing. Screening seven additional CYP fifty-one inhibitors in their T-D-P forty-three mislocalization-and-aggregation assay identified posaconazole as the strongest hit. Mechanism work shows posaconazole outperforms ketoconazole by inducing stronger autophagy activation and heat-shock-protein up-regulation that clear T-D-P forty-three, and its effect depends on its known ability to lower cellular cholesterol; predicted binding affinity to human CYP fifty-one across the panel correlates with potency against T-D-P forty-three. In a low-dose sodium-arsenite stress model in induced-pluripotent-stem-cell-derived motor neurons, posaconazole was active in the nanomolar range — a clinically deliverable, central-nervous-system-penetrant repurposing path for sporadic and familial T-D-P-forty-three proteinopathies.

Fourth, from Ellen Shrock, Eric Sun and colleagues with Stephen Elledge at Harvard and David Baker at the Institute for Protein Design, a systems-scale structural model showing that germline-encoded antibody features, not somatic mutations, primarily determine which antigenic sites become immunodominant. Using the SARS-CoV-two receptor-binding domain as the model antigen, the team combined large-scale antibody sequencing with deep mutational scanning and AlphaFold three structural modeling. Seventy-six percent of receptor-binding-domain-targeting antibodies share conserved gene-segment usage or germline-encoded heavy-chain C-D-R-three motifs, and structural analysis pinpoints recurrent germline residues that contact the immunodominant epitopes — mutating these residues abolishes binding. SARS-CoV-two variant substitutions frequently disrupt these germline contacts and explain immune escape; other variant substitutions open new germline-mediated epitopes, generating new immunodominant clusters. Innate genetic features dominate binding specificity for most of the antibody response — a constraint that rewrites how vaccine designers should think about epitope steering and germline targeting.

That's the brief. Four reasons today to be optimistic about cross-linked volumetric D-N-A microscopy as a sequencer-only substrate for dense three-dimensional molecular phenotyping, expression-increasing antisense oligonucleotides against regulatory R-N-As as a translatable modality for haploinsufficient disease at the L-D-L receptor locus, posaconazole as a clinically deliverable C-N-S-penetrant clearance agent for T-D-P-forty-three proteinopathies, and a systems-scale germline architecture of immunodominance that constrains how the antibody repertoire engages new antigens. Until tomorrow.
