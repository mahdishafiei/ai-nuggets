Good morning. It's Friday, June twelfth, twenty twenty-six, and this is your Calibr-Skaggs Daily Briefing. The lead story today is Takeda's LATITUDE Atlas Phase three head-to-head readout, in which zasocitinib — the selective allosteric T-Y-K-two inhibitor designed at Nimbus Therapeutics with Schrödinger's computational platform and acquired by Takeda for four billion dollars in December twenty twenty-four — was statistically superior to Bristol Myers Squibb's Sotyktu on every major endpoint in moderate-to-severe plaque psoriasis. That readout is today's spotlight. Six items in the roundup.

First, the Takeda zasocitinib data. In LATITUDE Atlas, the head-to-head Phase three study against deucravacitinib — Bristol Myers Squibb's branded Sotyktu — more than thirty-five percent of patients on the once-daily thirty-milligram dose of zasocitinib achieved P-A-S-I one hundred, complete skin clearance, at week sixteen. That is more than two-and-a-half times the complete-clearance rate Sotyktu produced at its approved six-milligram once-daily dose. Takeda also reported statistical superiority over Sotyktu on every key secondary endpoint, including P-A-S-I ninety and the static Physician's Global Assessment score of zero, with no new safety signals. The company plans to submit a U-S new-drug application this fiscal year, with detailed data at upcoming medical meetings. Sotyktu, the first F-D-A-approved oral T-Y-K-two inhibitor, generated two hundred ninety-one million dollars in twenty twenty-five against initial peak-sales expectations north of two billion, and Bristol Myers earlier this year announced it would stop actively promoting Sotyktu in dermatology in many markets. The spotlight in a few hours unpacks the chemistry — why allosteric binding at the J-H-two pseudokinase regulatory domain solves the J-A-K-family selectivity problem — and the discovery platform that took zasocitinib from a Nimbus and Schrödinger computational-chemistry project to a four-billion-dollar Takeda asset and now a Phase three head-to-head win.

Second, Novartis delivered the first major readout from the twelve-billion-dollar Avidity Biosciences acquisition. The FORTITUDE Phase one-and-two biomarker cohort for delpacibart braxlosiran — del-brax — hit its primary endpoint in facioscapulohumeral muscular dystrophy. Del-brax is an antibody-oligonucleotide conjugate, or A-O-C, that pairs a small interfering R-N-A targeting the DUX-four messenger R-N-A with an antibody to the transferrin receptor for delivery into skeletal muscle. Across fifty-one patients in the biomarker cohort, the drug significantly reduced both K-H-D-C-one — the circulating DUX-four-regulated biomarker — and creatine kinase, consistent with target engagement and reduced muscle damage. Novartis chief executive Vas Narasimhan said the company will discuss seeking accelerated approval with the F-D-A based on the Phase one-and-two data, in parallel with the ongoing Phase three confirmatory study that began enrolling last year and measures muscle strength via the ten-meter walk-and-run test through week seventy-eight. The Avidity transaction was the platform play of last year's M-and-A cycle, and this is the first asset-level validation event for the antibody-oligonucleotide conjugate modality under Novartis ownership. For Calibr, the read-through is to in-vivo targeted delivery — the A-O-C architecture uses an antibody-anchored receptor-mediated route to deliver an oligonucleotide payload into a specific cell type, which is the same conceptual structure as in-vivo CAR-T delivery and as the broader cell-targeted-delivery problem that anchors several Calibr-adjacent platforms.

Third, Johnson and Johnson presented the first comprehensive Phase two-and-three ENERGY data for Imaavy — nipocalimab — in warm autoimmune hemolytic anemia. Patients on the thirty-milligram-per-kilogram intravenous dose achieved roughly three times the rate of durable hemoglobin response versus placebo at week twenty-four, with mean hemoglobin improvements of at least one gram per deciliter as early as week one. Nipocalimab blocks the neonatal F-c receptor to reduce circulating I-g-G autoantibodies without depleting B-cells or disrupting other immune pathways. The F-D-A granted Priority Review on the supplemental B-L-A for warm autoimmune hemolytic anemia in April, and there is no F-D-A-approved therapy in this indication today, so an approval would make J-and-J's nipocalimab the first-in-class agent for warm autoimmune hemolytic anemia — layering on top of the generalized myasthenia gravis approval secured last year and reinforcing F-c-R-n inhibition as a multi-indication autoimmune platform franchise.

Fourth, Genentech executed another round of research-and-development restructuring overnight. Vice president and senior fellow Vishva Dixit, who joined Genentech in nineteen ninety-seven and has been one of the central figures in the company's apoptosis and cell-death biology for nearly thirty years, is among those affected. Other notable departures include cell-therapy head Todd McDevitt and infectious-disease senior fellow Man-Wah Tan. The infectious-disease and physiological-chemistry research units are closing entirely, with downsizing across early clinical development, development sciences, and translational medicine. Genentech declined to give a total headcount number. The combination of senior-figure departures across cell therapy and infectious diseases and the elimination of physiological chemistry — historically one of Genentech's foundational small-molecule capabilities — is the bigger signal than the raw layoff number. Genentech's parent Roche is one of the three Big Pharma names underwriting the Orionis Allo-Glue molecular-glue platform we covered yesterday, and the larger Genentech research footprint is contracting toward focused therapeutic areas around oncology, neuroscience, and immunology while externalizing the modality-discovery work.

Fifth, Princeton-based cardiology biotech Kardigan set terms for its Nasdaq I-P-O — twenty-three-point-three million shares at fourteen-to-sixteen dollars, raising roughly three hundred twenty million dollars at the midpoint with potential to reach three hundred seventy million if underwriters exercise the over-allotment option. Kardigan would be the thirteenth biotech I-P-O of twenty twenty-six, in a market where ten companies have already raised nearly three-point-two billion dollars year-to-date and six have priced above three hundred million. The company's late-stage pipeline includes three programs — danicamtiv, an oral cardiac myosin activator in Phase two-b-slash-three for genetic dilated cardiomyopathy driven by M-Y-H-seven and T-T-N mutations; ataciguat, an oral soluble guanylate cyclase activator in Phase two-b for calcific aortic valve stenosis progression; and tonlamarsen, a once-monthly subcutaneous antisense oligonucleotide targeting hepatic angiotensinogen for blood-pressure control in post-hospitalization acute severe hypertension. The cardiology-IPO signal is the more interesting takeaway here — public-market appetite for late-stage cardiology assets has been thin for years, and Kardigan's three-asset portfolio across genetic cardiomyopathy, structural heart disease, and resistant hypertension lands at the same time the obesity-and-cardiometabolic franchise is becoming the most-valuable category in pharma. The thirteen-IPO pace and the asset mix together signal that the cardiometabolic and cardiology windows are opening together for the first time in this cycle.

Sixth and last, Summit Therapeutics pulled the five-hundred-million-dollar secondary share sale it had announced one day earlier, citing market conditions. The retraction follows a mixed week for ivonescimab — Summit's bispecific P-D-one-by-V-E-G-F antibody co-developed with Akeso. Positive China-based Phase three lung-cancer data presented at ASCO twenty twenty-six lifted sentiment, but a separate global Phase three combining ivonescimab with Merck's Keytruda in frontline lung cancer failed to beat Keytruda alone at the early interim. Summit retains roughly six hundred million dollars in cash but the late-stage development burn is significant, and the cancelled raise resets the financing calendar against a twenty twenty-seven cash horizon.
