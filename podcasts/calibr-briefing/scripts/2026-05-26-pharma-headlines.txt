Good morning Travis. This is your Calibr-Skaggs Daily Briefing for Tuesday, May 26, 2026. Six headlines today.

First, Lilly. Late yesterday, Lilly announced Phase 1b Heart-2 data for VERVE-102, the in vivo PCSK9 base editor it inherited from the Verve Therapeutics acquisition last summer. A single intravenous infusion produced mean PCSK9 reductions ranging from fifty-one percent to eighty-eight percent across six dose cohorts, with LDL cholesterol reductions reaching sixty-two percent at the top one-milligram-per-kilogram dose. Effects sustained out to eighteen months. The data hit as a late-breaking oral at the European Atherosclerosis Society Congress and was published simultaneously in the New England Journal of Medicine. Lilly says Phase 2 launches by year end. We go deeper on the mechanism, the strategic implications, and the read-through for in vivo cell therapy in today's spotlight.

Second, Innovent. The Chinese biotech disclosed updated data on IBI363, a first-in-class PD-1 cross IL-2-alpha-bias bispecific fusion protein, in two ASCO 2026 abstracts. In long-term follow-up of immunotherapy-resistant non-small-cell lung cancer, IBI363 monotherapy showed durable survival signals. A separate abstract reports preliminary proof-of-concept data combining IBI363 with chemotherapy as first-line treatment for PD-L1-negative or low-expression NSCLC, with encouraging response rates. This is structurally different from the pumitamig and ivonescimab PD-axis-times-VEGF bispecifics we covered yesterday. IBI363 instead pairs PD-1 blockade with an alpha-bias IL-2 cytokine arm engineered to expand stem-like CD8 T cells while avoiding the toxicity of broad IL-2 activation. If the combination signal holds in larger trials, it opens an alternative bispecific lane for IO-resistant tumors that the VEGF-bispecifics do not address.

Third, Bristol Myers Squibb. SUCCESSOR-2, the first Phase 3 readout for mezigdomide, will present at ASCO this week. Mezigdomide is a next-generation cereblon E3 ligase modulator, the molecular glue class that includes lenalidomide and pomalidomide. The trial tested mezigdomide as an add-on to Amgen's Kyprolis plus dexamethasone in relapsed-refractory multiple myeloma. Positive topline was previously announced; the abstract release confirmed magnitude of progression-free survival benefit. BMS is positioning mezigdomide as the post-lenalidomide-resistant CELMoD backbone, with combinations across daratumumab, bispecifics, and CAR-T also reading out. This matters as a template — a chemically optimized molecular glue replacing an older class member while preserving the combination chassis.

Fourth, Summit and Akeso. The HARMONi-6 trial of ivonescimab plus chemotherapy versus Tevimbra plus chemotherapy in first-line squamous non-small-cell lung cancer takes a Plenary Session slot at ASCO on Sunday May 31. Ivonescimab is the PD-1 cross VEGF-A bispecific that previously beat pembrolizumab monotherapy in the HARMONi-2 readout. HARMONi-6 is the first head-to-head against Tevimbra, the Chinese PD-1 antibody, with chemotherapy backbones on both arms. Plenary placement signals practice-changing magnitude. This continues to detonate the field — pumitamig and ivonescimab are both pushing into Keytruda combination territory, and the question is which gets to US registrational filings first.

Fifth, GSK and Boston Pharmaceuticals. ASCO is the oncology meeting but worth flagging the MASH update — efimosfermin alfa, the once-monthly long-acting FGF21 analogue GSK acquired from Boston for two billion dollars last May, has Phase 2 twenty-four-week data showing forty-five percent achieved at-least-one-stage fibrosis improvement versus twenty-one percent on placebo, and sixty-eight percent achieved MASH resolution versus twenty-nine percent. GSK is moving toward Phase 3 against an efruxifermin backdrop that includes Novo Nordisk's 5.2-billion-dollar acquisition of Akero last October. The monthly-dosing frequency advantage versus weekly efruxifermin is the differentiator. Two FGF21 analogues, two billion-dollar-plus deals, both heading into pivotal trials — the FGF21 mechanism is now the dominant non-resmetirom MASH thesis, which matters for Calibr's dual-acting FGF21-based MASH biologic in IND-enabling work.
