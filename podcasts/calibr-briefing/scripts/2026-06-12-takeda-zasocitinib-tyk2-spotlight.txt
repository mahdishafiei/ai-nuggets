Welcome back. The spotlight today is the Takeda LATITUDE Atlas Phase three readout for zasocitinib, the once-daily oral selective allosteric T-Y-K-two inhibitor that delivered more than two-and-a-half times the complete-skin-clearance rate of Bristol Myers Squibb's approved oral T-Y-K-two inhibitor Sotyktu in a head-to-head trial in moderate-to-severe plaque psoriasis. The story is the chemistry, the computational-discovery platform that produced the molecule, the competitive landscape the readout reshapes, and the read-through to Calibr-Skaggs. Four threads.

The first thread is the chemistry — what selective allosteric T-Y-K-two inhibition actually is, and why it is hard. T-Y-K-two is one of four members of the Janus kinase family, alongside J-A-K-one, J-A-K-two, and J-A-K-three. Each family member sits beneath a defined cluster of interleukin and interferon cytokine receptors and signals through the downstream S-T-A-T transcription factors. T-Y-K-two in particular sits beneath the I-L-twelve, I-L-twenty-three, and type-one-interferon pathways — the cytokine axis that drives plaque psoriasis, psoriatic arthritis, lupus, and a number of other immune-mediated diseases — which is what makes it a desirable immunology target. Structurally, each J-A-K-family kinase has two adjacent domains. The J-H-one domain is the catalytic kinase that binds A-T-P and phosphorylates downstream substrates. The J-H-two domain is an inactive pseudokinase regulatory domain that sits next to J-H-one and structurally constrains its activity. The vast majority of kinase inhibitors in clinical use today bind the A-T-P pocket of the J-H-one catalytic domain — that pocket is well-characterized, drug-like, and easy to access by conventional medicinal chemistry. The problem for the J-A-K family specifically is that the J-H-one A-T-P pocket is highly conserved across J-A-K-one, J-A-K-two, J-A-K-three, and T-Y-K-two, which means A-T-P-competitive inhibitors at typical clinical exposures tend to hit multiple family members. That brings the J-A-K-two-driven hematology liabilities — anemia, neutropenia, thrombocytosis — and the J-A-K-three-driven immune-cell defects into therapeutic-dose ranges in immunology indications, where the dose ceilings of J-A-K-one-and-three inhibitors like tofacitinib, baricitinib, and upadacitinib are constrained by these off-target effects. The boxed warning on systemic oral J-A-K inhibitors for major adverse cardiovascular events, thromboembolism, malignancy, and serious infections is in large part a consequence of that off-target pharmacology. Selective T-Y-K-two inhibition is, in principle, the way to escape that class warning — but only if you can bind T-Y-K-two at a site that is structurally distinct from the highly conserved J-H-one A-T-P pocket. Sotyktu — Bristol Myers's deucravacitinib — and Takeda's zasocitinib both do this by binding the J-H-two pseudokinase regulatory domain rather than the J-H-one catalytic A-T-P pocket. The J-H-two domain is structurally more divergent across the J-A-K family, which is what makes selectivity achievable. A ligand bound at J-H-two does not directly block A-T-P engagement; instead, it allosterically stabilizes the kinase in an inactive conformation. The end result is pathway inhibition with vastly improved selectivity over the J-A-K paralogs and, in principle, escape from the J-A-K class warning. The execution challenge is that allosteric pockets are harder to find, harder to design ligands against, and harder to drive to picomolar potency than orthosteric A-T-P sites. That is the chemistry problem that zasocitinib solves.

The second thread is the platform that produced it. Zasocitinib was discovered at Nimbus Therapeutics, the computational-chemistry-anchored discovery company that Atlas Venture's Bruce Booth and Schrödinger chief executive Ramy Farid co-founded in two thousand nine. The Nimbus founding thesis was simple and contrarian — physics-based computational simulations could displace empirical trial-and-error in drug design, and a small team paired with the right computational platform could produce assets that a much larger conventional medicinal-chemistry shop could not. Beginning in twenty sixteen, Nimbus chemists and Schrödinger computational chemists teamed up on the T-Y-K-two problem. They first tried the catalytic J-H-one domain, then pivoted to the J-H-two pseudokinase regulatory domain after structural analysis suggested the selectivity prospects were materially better. The technical hurdle was predicting binding affinity to T-Y-K-two with sufficient accuracy to drive selectivity over J-A-K-two and J-A-K-three by orders of magnitude — a calculation that is computationally expensive and historically unreliable for allosteric binding. Schrödinger applied F-E-P-plus — free-energy perturbation calculations — at large scale to predict both on-target T-Y-K-two potency and off-target potency at J-A-K-two and J-A-K-three for every candidate chemical modification, allowing the medicinal chemists to triage virtual compounds and synthesize only those predicted to widen the selectivity window. The crystal-structure work, A-I-driven binding-pose modeling, and iterative wet-lab screening were tightly integrated with synthesis decisions across a multi-year medicinal-chemistry campaign. The molecule that emerged — first called N-D-I-zero-three-four-eight-five-eight, then T-A-K-two-seven-nine, now zasocitinib — was an allosteric T-Y-K-two inhibitor with picomolar on-target potency and three-to-four orders of magnitude of selectivity over J-A-K-two and J-A-K-three. Takeda licensed the asset out of Nimbus in December twenty twenty-two and then acquired the standalone zasocitinib program outright in December twenty twenty-four for four billion dollars — the largest single-asset deal of that year. The LATITUDE Atlas head-to-head readout this week is the validation event not just for the molecule but for the entire Nimbus-and-Schrödinger platform thesis. It is the cleanest demonstration to date that physics-based computational chemistry paired with traditional medicinal chemistry can deliver a best-in-class small molecule at a target where conventional structure-based design had hit a selectivity wall.

The third thread is the competitive landscape the readout reshapes. Bristol Myers Squibb's Sotyktu — deucravacitinib, launched in twenty twenty-two as the first F-D-A-approved oral T-Y-K-two inhibitor — was supposed to be a blockbuster pipeline asset for B-M-S. The clinical proposition was compelling: an oral pill with the efficacy of the I-L-twenty-three injectable biologics, without the boxed J-A-K-class warnings of the J-A-K-one-and-three inhibitors. The launch did not deliver. Sotyktu generated two hundred ninety-one million dollars in twenty twenty-five against initial peak-sales expectations north of two billion dollars. The execution problem was a combination of access friction — payers steered patients toward injectable biologics with established outcomes data — and a real efficacy ceiling. Sotyktu's P-A-S-I one hundred rate at the approved six-milligram dose at week sixteen sat in the mid-teens, well below the response rates of injectable I-L-twenty-three biologics like risankizumab and guselkumab, which generally hit thirty-to-forty percent complete clearance at the same time point. Dermatologists noticed. Bristol Myers earlier this year said it would stop actively promoting Sotyktu in dermatology across multiple markets, conceding the segment. Zasocitinib lands into that gap with a clean competitive proposition. The thirty-five-plus-percent P-A-S-I one hundred rate at week sixteen materially outperforms Sotyktu and approaches the early-window response rates of injectable I-L-twenty-three biologics, with the additional benefit of being an oral pill at full daily compliance economics. Takeda chief executive-elect Julie Kim called zasocitinib poised to be the leading oral treatment option in psoriasis. The company is already running pivotals in psoriatic arthritis head-to-head against deucravacitinib, and Phase two studies in Crohn's disease, ulcerative colitis, vitiligo, and hidradenitis suppurativa are in motion. The competitive question for the next twenty-four months is whether selective T-Y-K-two inhibition can match injectable biologics on long-term durability and breadth across the broader immunology landscape. What is already settled this week is that the first-in-class oral T-Y-K-two slot has been re-priced. The platform thesis behind zasocitinib's discovery has also been re-priced. Nimbus chose to monetize the T-Y-K-two asset rather than build a company around it — the common exit path for computational-chemistry-anchored discovery shops — but the success of the asset directly validates Schrödinger's F-E-P-plus thesis across the broader kinase-selectivity universe, and Nimbus's remaining pipeline plus Schrödinger's standalone platform business should benefit.

The fourth thread is the read-through to Calibr-Skaggs. Three connections. First, on the discovery-platform thesis. The pattern this week has been remarkably consistent. Monday it was Nurix-and-Roche on the bexobrutideg B-T-K degrader, validating Nurix's heterobifunctional-degrader platform at a billion-dollar comparable. Wednesday it was the Parabilis I-P-O at seven hundred forty-five million dollars, validating the Helicon stapled-peptide platform for intracellular protein-protein interactions. Thursday it was Novartis-and-Orionis at one-point-four billion dollars in milestone value, validating the Allo-Glue monovalent-molecular-glue platform. Friday it is Takeda's LATITUDE Atlas head-to-head readout, validating the Nimbus-and-Schrödinger physics-based selective-kinase platform. The pattern is that the A-I and computational platforms drawing billion-dollar industry validation events are not general-purpose chemistry-from-scratch engines. They are platforms anchored on a specific high-value chemistry problem — kinase allosteric selectivity, induced proximity, intracellular protein-protein interactions, stapled-peptide membrane penetration — where the computational machinery has demonstrable purchase against a clearly defined biology problem. The lesson for how Calibr scopes its own computational-chemistry investments is that the capability that scales is the one paired with a concrete chemical-biology readout and a defined target-class problem, not generic in-silico tooling. Second, on the chemistry itself. The J-H-two-domain allosteric strategy is not specific to T-Y-K-two. Many of the targets Calibr cares about across inflammation, autoimmunity, and cancer biology have pseudokinase regulatory domains, allosteric cryptic pockets, or scaffolding interfaces where selectivity could be designed in rather than relying on the orthosteric A-T-P pocket. The F-E-P-plus-driven selectivity-prediction workflow that Nimbus and Schrödinger used is now a validated playbook for kinase-family selectivity problems, and the technical stack — F-E-P-plus plus crystal structures plus iterative medicinal chemistry — is within reach for academically-anchored chemistry organizations working with the right computational collaborators. The barrier to entry is access to high-quality structural biology and the willingness to commit to a multi-year medicinal-chemistry campaign — both of which Calibr has. Third, on oral immunology positioning. The success of zasocitinib at the selective-allosteric-T-Y-K-two locus tells the market that the oral immunology landscape will continue to fragment along selectivity-and-mechanism lines, with each major immune cytokine pathway potentially supporting a best-in-class small molecule. For Calibr programs that intersect immune cytokine signaling — and the MASH biologic operates partly through immune-and-metabolic crosstalk on F-G-F-twenty-one and related axes — the implication is that the J-A-K-and-T-Y-K-two-family allosteric-selectivity playbook should sit on the menu of design strategies whenever the underlying biology supports it. Even where the modality is biologic rather than small-molecule, the design principle — pick a structurally divergent regulatory pocket rather than the conserved active site — translates directly.

The bottom line: zasocitinib's LATITUDE Atlas Phase three head-to-head readout is the largest single validation event for physics-based computational chemistry in two years. The thirty-five-plus-percent P-A-S-I one hundred response at week sixteen places zasocitinib above Sotyktu by more than two-and-a-half times, repositions the first-in-class oral T-Y-K-two slot for Takeda's filing this fiscal year, and resets the financial comparable for selective allosteric kinase assets discovered by Schrödinger-style physics-first platforms. The J-H-two-domain allosteric strategy generalizes beyond T-Y-K-two to a broad class of kinase-selectivity problems. The Nimbus-and-Schrödinger platform thesis — physics-and-F-E-P-plus-led discovery anchored on a specific high-value chemistry problem — is the configuration that has drawn billion-dollar industry validation across all four modality clusters covered on this briefing this week. And the oral-immunology landscape that zasocitinib now defines is the strategic playbook Calibr should keep close whenever a target's biology supports selective allosteric design.
