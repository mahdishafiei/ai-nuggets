Headline today is Merck-K-G-a-A out of Darmstadt agreeing yesterday to acquire Bio-Techne for eleven-point-three-billion dollars in cash — the largest deal Merck-K-G-a-A has done in eleven years, since the seventeen-billion-dollar Sigma-Aldrich purchase in twenty-fourteen, and the largest move on life-science-tools-and-reagents M-and-A in the post-pandemic cycle. First — and I'll go deep on it — Merck-K-G-a-A is paying seventy-three dollars per share in cash for Bio-Techne, an enterprise value of eleven-point-three-billion-dollars, a thirty-six-percent premium to Bio-Techne's one-month volume-weighted-average trading price. Bio-Techne is the Minneapolis-headquartered life-science-tools company that owns the R-and-D Systems reagent franchise, the RNAscope spatial-biology platform from the twenty-sixteen Advanced Cell Diagnostics acquisition, the Lunaphore multiplexed-imaging platform from twenty twenty-three, ProteinSimple instruments, and a nineteen-point-nine-percent stake in Wilson Wolf with a forward contract to acquire the rest after twenty twenty-seven for the cell-therapy bioreactor and culture-media business. Fiscal-year twenty-twenty-five revenue was more than one-point-two-billion-dollars. Goldman is on both sides; Merck-K-G-a-A is financing with cash and new debt and committing to maintain its investment-grade rating. About one-hundred-forty-million euros in cost synergies promised by year three, immediately EBITDA-pre-margin accretive, EPS accretive in year three. Expected close late twenty-twenty-six or early twenty-twenty-seven. The deal is Merck-K-G-a-A's response to the structural pressure Thermo Fisher, Danaher, and Sartorius have been applying on the tools-and-reagents stack — Thermo Fisher's four-point-one-billion-dollar Solventum purification-and-filtration deal last February, Danaher's Genedata bioinformatics tuck-in in twenty twenty-four. The spotlight goes into what this means for the AI-drug-discovery and cell-and-gene-therapy infrastructure layer that Calibr and Foresite portfolio companies depend on.

Second, Ionis Pharmaceuticals scored a major FDA label expansion for Tryngolza — olezarsen — Tuesday evening. The approved indication moves from familial chylomicronemia syndrome, the ultra-rare monogenic form, to the broad severe-hypertriglyceridemia population, which Ionis estimates at more than three-million U.S. patients. Tryngolza is the first and only therapy approved to reduce both triglycerides and the risk of acute pancreatitis in that population. The pivotal CORE and CORE-two trials showed placebo-adjusted fasting-triglyceride reductions of roughly forty-nine to sixty-three percent at the fifty-milligram dose and fifty-five to seventy-two percent at the eighty-milligram dose at six months, sustained out to twelve months, with a pooled acute-pancreatitis rate ratio of zero-point-one-five at twelve months, p less than zero-point-zero-zero-one, and eighty-six percent of patients achieving triglycerides below five-hundred milligrams per deciliter. Olezarsen is an antisense oligonucleotide against APOC-three messenger-R-N-A; it's the original Ionis-platform asset that came back from the Akcea joint-venture restructuring. Self-administered once monthly subcutaneous autoinjector, U.S. availability in July. Wall Street is calling this Ionis's commercial inflection point — the move from Akcea-era rare-disease single-indication to a broad cardiovascular population. The competitive read sits against Arrowhead's plozasiran in the same APOC-three space and Novo Nordisk's RNAi positioning.

Third, Otsuka posted a positive Phase three-b readout for centanafadine in adults with A-D-H-D and comorbid anxiety. Three-hundred-fifteen patients, randomized double-blind placebo-controlled, two-hundred-eighty milligrams once daily, hit the primary endpoint on the Adult Investigator Symptom Rating Scale at week eight, with consistent improvements on executive function and emotional dysregulation. The NDA — which covers children, adolescents, and adults — has FDA Priority Review with a PDUFA date of July twenty-fourth. Centanafadine is a first-in-class triple-reuptake inhibitor — norepinephrine, dopamine, and serotonin — and it's the cleanest non-stimulant differentiation story heading into a market where the stimulant supply chain remains structurally constrained and where comorbid anxiety is the single most common reason patients drop stimulants. The yesterday readout is positioned as comorbidity evidence to support label-and-payer positioning post-approval.

Fourth, Boehringer Ingelheim signed a fifteen-million-dollar discovery collaboration with Immunai, the New York single-cell A-I immunology platform. The collaboration spans immuno-oncology and autoimmune disease — the same T-cell-dysfunction biology applied across both, using Immunai's AMICA-O-S operating system to map immune-cell-state landscapes from thousands of patient samples and identify new T-cell targets. Two-year initial term running through twenty twenty-seven with expansion optionality. The structural framing is that Immunai now has three big-pharma collaborations signed inside six months — Bristol-Myers Squibb in January for oncology, the third expansion of its AstraZeneca multi-year partnership in May, and now Boehringer. Immunai has raised about two-hundred-seventy-million-dollars to date. The read for Calibr's and Foresite's I-O and autoimmune exposure is that the immune-systems-modeling layer of A-I drug discovery — distinct from the protein-design layer at Xaira and Iambic — is consolidating around a few platforms with multi-pharma data leverage, and the per-deal price point is staying below twenty-million on upfront economics.
