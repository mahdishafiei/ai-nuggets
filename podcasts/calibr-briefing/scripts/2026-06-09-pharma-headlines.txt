Good morning. It's Tuesday, June ninth, twenty twenty-six, and this is your Calibr-Skaggs Daily Briefing. The lead story today is a ten-point-six-billion-dollar M and A announcement out of London — G-S-K is buying Nuvalent, the precision-oncology company whose two N-S-C-L-C assets are sitting under F-D-A review with action dates in the second half of this year. That's the largest oncology transaction G-S-K has done in more than a decade and is today's spotlight. Six items in the roundup.

First, the deal itself. G-S-K and Nuvalent announced this morning that G-S-K will acquire Nuvalent for approximately ten-point-six billion dollars in cash, or roughly one hundred twenty-four dollars per share — a forty-percent premium to Friday's close. Net of cash acquired, the aggregate investment is around nine-point-four billion dollars, primarily debt-funded. G-S-K is buying two clinical-stage R-O-S-one and A-L-K inhibitors for non-small-cell lung cancer, both of which have completed pivotal trials and sit under F-D-A priority review with PDUFA dates in September and November of this year. C-E-O Luke Miels framed the transaction as a step-change for the oncology franchise. This is Miels's largest deal since taking the chief-executive role and follows the two-point-two-billion-dollar RAPT Therapeutics acquisition in January. Sales and operating-profit accretion are guided to twenty twenty-seven and core E-P-S accretion to twenty twenty-nine. Spotlight goes deep on what G-S-K is actually buying, how it slots against the Roche and Pfizer R-O-S-one and A-L-K franchises, and what the transaction signals about precision-oncology valuations.

Second, Johnson and Johnson announced yesterday afternoon a one-billion-dollar all-cash acquisition of Firefly Bio. Firefly is a Series-A-stage company that came out of Versant Ventures in twenty twenty-two and has raised roughly one hundred million dollars in venture funding. The asset is the Firelink degrader-antibody-conjugate platform — antibody-conjugated targeted protein degraders, where the antibody delivers a degrader payload selectively to tumor tissue instead of the conventional cytotoxic payload used in classical antibody-drug conjugates. The lead programs are pre-clinical and aimed at K-R-A-S-driven solid tumors. This is J and J's second oncology bolt-on with an A-D-C-platform angle since the two-billion-dollar Ambrx purchase in early twenty twenty-four, and it lands two weeks after the spotlight we ran on the targeted-protein-degradation modality. The degrader-A-D-C concept is one of the cleanest convergences of the two highest-growth modalities in oncology — protein degradation for the catalytic mechanism, antibody-conjugate delivery for the tissue selectivity. A one-billion-dollar all-cash check for a preclinical platform is the price degrader-modality assets now command.

Third, also out yesterday: Incyte announced a definitive agreement to acquire Vega Therapeutics from Star Therapeutics for one-point-two-five billion dollars upfront, with up to seven hundred fifty million dollars in sales milestones, for a total potential value of two billion dollars. The asset is V-G-A-zero-three-nine — a subcutaneous monoclonal antibody in Phase three pivotal development for von Willebrand disease, the most common inherited bleeding disorder. The clinical premise is to replace the intermittent intravenous factor infusions that V-W-D patients currently rely on with a subcutaneous prophylactic dosed less frequently. Incyte is expanding its hematology franchise beyond myeloproliferative neoplasms, where Jakafi has carried the company for a decade, and beyond its more recent atopic dermatitis push. Expected close is third quarter of this year.

Fourth, Tango Therapeutics and Revolution Medicines reported a first look at a combination dataset in pancreatic cancer that drew analyst attention yesterday. The combination pairs Tango's vopimetostat — a P-R-M-T-five inhibitor selective for tumors with M-T-A-P deletion, which is present in roughly twenty to thirty-seven percent of pancreatic adenocarcinomas — with Revolution Medicines' daraxonrasib, the multi-on R-A-S inhibitor that read out positive Phase three pancreatic-cancer data the week before at A-S-C-O. Of twelve patients on the combination who had progressed on prior therapy and had at least fourteen weeks of follow-up, eleven responded, for a ninety-two percent response rate. The analyst-consult bar for "meaningful" was fifty to sixty percent, so this materially overshot expectations. Toxicities included rash and mouth sores, with two enrollees on the highest vopimetostat dose hitting severe adverse events. This is a small dataset and an interim cut, but the response rate is the most striking R-A-S-plus-synthetic-lethal-partner readout the field has seen, and it's the first hard signal that the Revolution Medicines pancreatic-cancer asset will support a combination strategy on top of monotherapy efficacy. Calibr has direct exposure here through the A-I-and-precision-oncology focus on Revolution Medicines.

Fifth, on the venture side, City Therapeutics closed a Series B of approximately one hundred million dollars yesterday. City is a next-generation R-N-A-i company founded by former Alnylam scientists working on chemical innovations that extend R-N-A-interference activity into tissues outside the liver, with a primary focus on central nervous system and extrahepatic targets. The round comes in a market where R-N-A-i and small-interfering-R-N-A funding has been concentrated on the small number of platforms that have crossed the in-vivo durability threshold — Alnylam, Arrowhead, Avidity. Calibr watches the broader oligonucleotide and R-N-A-therapeutic environment for both modality and venture-formation signals, and this is one of the cleaner ones in the modality this year.

Sixth, two other items worth a note. Bial discontinued the clinical development of its lead Parkinson's-disease asset this morning after a Phase two miss, a setback for a smaller European pipeline. And Pfizer received F-D-A approval for an expanded pediatric indication for Hympavzi, its T-F-P-I-targeting subcutaneous hemophilia A and B therapy, broadening the addressable population for the franchise that competes against the BioMarin gene therapies and the Sanofi efanesoctocog alfa.
